Walter White

Former Caltech Chemical Enginnering PHD student, School Chemistry teacher, and Meth Cook

Bio Blurb

Walter White's life was a gripping journey of ambition, sacrifice, and moral descent. Born in humble circumstances, he displayed early brilliance in chemistry, attending the prestigious California Institute of Technology. However, life's hardships steered him away from a promising career, leading him to become a high school chemistry teacher in Albuquerque, New Mexico.

Struggling with financial woes and a devastating cancer diagnosis, Walter's desperation pushed him to a fateful decision - manufacturing and selling methamphetamine. Adopting the alias "heisenberg" , named after the famous German scientist Werner Heisenberg, he evolved into a cunning and ruthless drug lord.

As his criminal empire grew, Walter's relationships unraveled, pitting him against former student and partner Jesse Pinkman and his own family. Trapped in a web of lies and violence, Walter's downfall was inevitable.

In the end, Walter's tumultuous journey concluded in a blaze of redemption and tragedy, leaving a legacy of both awe and terror, forever changing the lives of those who knew him.

Facts about Walter white

Walter White first started his methamphetamine bussiness in an RV called a "Fleetwood Bounder" that was produced in the mid-late 80's and was nicknamed the "The Krystal ship" by Jesse Pinkman and a few of his friends.

Walter White then worked at an underground meth lab disguised as a laundry, this location can be found at 1613 Candelaria Rd NE, Albequerque New Mexico

Walter White's own son, Walter Jr , created a website for him in order to raise money for his father, unaware of his drug business.
